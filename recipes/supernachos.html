Super Nachos

Super Nachos are a mouthwatering combination of crispy tortilla chips topped with seasoned ground beef, melted cheese, diced tomatoes, sliced jalapeños, black olives, sour cream, and guacamole. This ultimate comfort food is perfect for parties, game nights, or any occasion when you want to enjoy a flavorful and satisfying snack.

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
Olive oil 1 tbsp.
Yellow onion, diced, divided 1
Ground beef 2 lb.
Chili powder 1/2 tsp.
Paprika 1/2 tsp.
Cumin 1/2 tsp.
Crushed red pepper 1/4 tsp.
Salt 1/2 tsp.
Ground black pepper 1/2 tsp.
Pinto beans, chili beans, or ranch style beans (canned) 1 can (14.5-oz.)
Hot water 1/2 c.
Roma tomatoes, diced 6
Jalapeño, diced finely 1
Cilantro leaves, chopped 1/2 bunch
Lime juice Juice of 1 lime
Salt 1/2 tsp.
Tortilla chips As needed
Grated cheddar cheese, divided 1 1/2 c.
Monterey jack cheese 1 1/2 c.
Avocado, pitted and diced 1
Sour cream (optional) As needed

Directions

  • Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  • Add half of the diced yellow onion to the skillet and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  • Add 2 pounds of ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it apart with a spoon as it cooks.
  • Season the beef with 1/2 teaspoon of chili powder, 1/2 teaspoon of paprika, 1/2 teaspoon of cumin, 1/4 teaspoon of crushed red pepper, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 teaspoon of ground black pepper. Stir well to combine.
  • Add 1 can (14.5 ounces) of pinto beans, chili beans, or ranch style beans to the skillet, along with 1/2 cup of hot water. Stir to combine and simmer for 10 minutes.
  • In a separate bowl, mix together the diced Roma tomatoes, finely diced jalapeño, chopped cilantro leaves, lime juice, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt. Set aside.
  • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  • Arrange a layer of tortilla chips on a baking sheet or oven-safe dish.
  • Sprinkle half of the grated cheddar cheese and half of the monterey jack cheese over the chips.
  • Spoon the beef and bean mixture over the cheese and chips.
  • Add another layer of chips on top of the beef mixture, followed by the remaining grated cheddar cheese and monterey jack cheese.
  • Bake in the preheated oven for 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  • Remove from the oven and top with the diced avocado and remaining diced yellow onion.
  • Serve immediately with sour cream on the side, if desired.
